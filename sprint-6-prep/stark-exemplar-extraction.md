## Drafting Patterns Observed

- **section_id convention:** `s{N}_{snake_case}` for body sections, `s{Letter}_{snake_case}` for appendices (sA_conflicts_register, sB_contact_details, sF1_order_records). TemplateDiscoveryAgent should follow this exactly.
- **Portability layers:** sections are tagged portability_layer 1-3 — layer 2 (the bulk) is jurisdiction-portable structural content; layer 1 is entity-specific, layer 3 is boilerplate.
- **Leverage category:** A/B/C grading on how reusable a section's pattern is across firms.
- **Frontmatter is the metadata contract:** every extracted section carries source_entity, source_jurisdiction, source_document, portability_layer, leverage_category, quality_score, word_count, citation_count.
- **Section text is body-only:** the '## Source Heading' + '## Section Text' split keeps the heading separate from the prose, so the template's `title` and the drafted `body` are cleanly separable.

## Translation to SectionSpec (for TemplateDiscoveryAgent — Option C)

Each Stark section maps to a SectionSpec as follows:
- `section_id` — take directly from the heading_map (already snake_case `s{N}_*`)
- `title` — the source heading text
- `required_provisions` — every regulatory citation in the section body (Stark sections average low citation_count; ADGM Cat-3C templates should cite more densely than Stark's extracted form, which lost inline citations during extraction)
- `drafting_instructions` — encode the section's structural pattern: how 'The Firm' is introduced, the intro→body→closing shape, the list-heavy vs prose-heavy style
- `min_word_count` — section actual word_count × 0.7 floor (Stark avg ~426 words/section)
- `portability_layer` — carry the Stark tagging directly; layer-2 sections are the cross-jurisdiction-portable ones
